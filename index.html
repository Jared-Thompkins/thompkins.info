    My journey at UIUC began as a transfer student in the midst of the pandemic (August 2020). At move-in, I shared a living space with 3 individuals whom I hadn't met before but who eventually became my lifelong friends. During my time at the university, I aimed to connect with as many of the 40,000 enrolled students as possible.

    UIUC is home to a diverse range of on-campus clubs and activities. I was a member of the National Association of Black Accountants (NABA) and also participated in intramural basketball, flag football, and even tried my hand at underwater hockey. During my senior year, I was fortunate to serve as a Resident Advisor (RA) . This role helped me learn a lot about myself and my own subconscious biases, as well as how to build community, lead events, and teach. I was also on call at times, gaining valuable experience in managing emergencies and addressing student needs.

    In addition to my extracurricular activities, I also formed strong connections with fellow students and professors in the classroom. Despite the pandemic, I chose to have as many of my classes as possible in-person, which allowed me to delve deeply into the class material.
